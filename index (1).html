Popular Attractions

Kathmandu

Discover ancient temples and vibrant markets in Nepal's capital. Nepal’s bustling capital, is a kaleidoscope of ancient traditions, modern life, and spiritual energy. This city is a gateway to Himalayan adventures and a sanctuary for cultural explorers.

Pokhara

Experience the serene beauty of lakes and mountains. Nepal’s bustling capital, is a kaleidoscope of ancient traditions, modern life, and spiritual energy. This city is a gateway to Himalayan adventures and a sanctuary for cultural explorers.

Bhaktapur

Step back in time as you wander through Bhaktapur, an ancient city renowned for its well-preserved medieval architecture and rich cultural heritage. Known as the "City of Devotees," Bhaktapur offers a mesmerizing blend of history and spirituality.

Patan

Patan or Lalitpur is Known as the "City of Fine Arts", Patan is famous for its rich history, intricate Newari architecture, and vibrant cultural heritage.

Lumbini

The birthplace of Lord Buddha, Lumbini is a sacred pilgrimage site and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Chitwan

A nature lover’s paradise, Chitwan is known for its lush jungles and diverse wildlife.

Key attractions

Boudhanath

One of the largest Buddhist stupas in the world, Boudhanath is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its massive white dome and all-seeing eyes symbolize peace and wisdom, attracting pilgrims and tourists alike.

Hanuman Dhoka

A historic palace complex located in Kathmandu Durbar Square, it once served as the royal residence. Named after the Hindu deity Hanuman, it features ancient courtyards and temples.

Garden of Dreams

A serene neo-classical garden in the heart of Kathmandu, known for its beautiful pavilions, fountains, and lush greenery. It’s a perfect escape from the city's hustle.

Asan Tole

A bustling market square, Asan is famous for its vibrant local trade, offering everything from spices and vegetables to traditional Nepali goods and souvenirs.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historic square is filled with ancient temples, palaces, and courtyards. It’s a cultural and architectural centerpiece of Kathmandu.

Kumari Bahal(Kumari Ghar)

A traditional Newari palace, home to the Kumari, the living goddess of Nepal. Known for its exquisite wooden carvings and spiritual significance.

Taleju Temple

Dedicated to the goddess Taleju Bhawani, this temple is one of the tallest and most revered in Kathmandu Durbar Square, only open to Hindus during specific festivals.

Golden Temple

A Buddhist monastery in Patan with intricate golden details and a peaceful courtyard. Known for its unique fusion of Buddhist and Hindu elements.

Krishna Mandir

A stunning stone temple in Patan Durbar Square, dedicated to Lord Krishna, known for its intricate carvings depicting scenes from Hindu epics.

Patan Durbar Square

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its Newari architecture, royal palaces, and artistic masterpieces. It’s the cultural heart of Patan.

Nyatapola

Nepal’s tallest pagoda, located in Bhaktapur Durbar Square. Dedicated to Siddhi Lakshmi, it’s known for its perfect symmetry and impressive stone guardians.

Golden Gate

A magnificent gilded entrance to the 55 Window Palace in Bhaktapur Durbar Square. Its intricate design is a prime example of Newari craftsmanship.

Lookout Tower

A popular spot in Nagarkot, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas, including Mount Everest on clear days. Ideal for sunrise and sunset photography.

Changu Narayan

The oldest temple in Nepal, located on a hilltop near Bhaktapur. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and features exquisite stone carvings.

Trekking

Trekking in Nepal offers an unparalleled experience of natural beauty, cultural diversity, and adventure. With its vast Himalayan range, including eight of the world’s 14 highest peaks, Nepal is a dream destination for trekkers of all levels.

Annapurna circuit trek

A classic trek renowned for its diverse landscapes—from subtropical forests to alpine deserts—and crossing the Thorong La Pass (5,416m). It showcases a blend of cultural experiences and panoramic views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges.

Makalu Base Camp trek

A challenging trek leading to the base of Mount Makalu, the fifth-highest peak in the world. It passes through lush forests, high-altitude terrains, and remote villages, offering solitude and stunning views of the Makalu-Barun National Park.

Poon hill trek

A short and rewarding trek offering stunning sunrise views over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges from Poon Hill (3,210m). It’s perfect for beginners and those seeking a quick Himalayan experience.

Langtang trek

Langtang offers a mix of dense forests, traditional Tamang villages, and breathtaking views of Langtang Lirung. It's an ideal trek for those seeking a shorter, yet rewarding, Himalayan experience.

Mardi Himal trek

A less crowded trek that takes you through rhododendron forests and rugged paths to the Mardi Himal Base Camp. It offers panoramic views of Annapurna, Machapuchare (Fishtail), and other peaks, perfect for nature lovers.

upper Mustang trek

A journey into the remote and arid landscapes of the Mustang region, known for its unique Tibetan culture, ancient monasteries, and dramatic desert-like scenery. Often referred to as the "Forbidden Kingdom," it offers a rare cultural experience.

Featured Packages

Annapurna Circuit trek(ABC)

A classic trek offering a diverse landscape, from subtropical forests to alpine terrain. It passes through charming villages, high mountain passes, and stunning views of Annapurna, Dhaulagiri, and Machapuchare.

Duration: 12-18 Days

Everest Base Camp trek (EBC)

The iconic trek takes you to the base of the world’s highest peak, Mt. Everest (8,848m). Along the way, trekkers experience Sherpa culture, monasteries, and breathtaking views of Everest, Lhotse, and Ama Dablam.

Duration: 12-14 Days

Poon Hill Trek

A short and relatively easy trek offering panoramic sunrise views over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges. Ideal for beginners, it passes through rhododendron forests, charming villages, and terraced farmlands.

Duration: 4-6 Days

Mardi Himal trek

A relatively short and less-crowded trek with breathtaking views of Annapurna, Machapuchare, and Mardi Himal. The trek passes through beautiful rhododendron forests and offers peaceful, scenic mountain views.

Duration: 5-7 Days

Manasulu trek

A challenging yet rewarding trek around the world’s eighth-highest peak, Mt. Manaslu (8,163m). This trek offers a remote wilderness experience with Tibetan-influenced villages, lush valleys, and high mountain passes.

Duration: 14-18 Days

Upper Mustang trek

A unique trek through the arid, desert-like landscapes of the former Mustang Kingdom. Trekkers can explore ancient monasteries, caves, and the culturally rich Lo Manthang, with Tibetan influence deeply rooted in the region.

Duration: 12-14 Days
